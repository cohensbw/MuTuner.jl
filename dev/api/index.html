MuTuner.MuTunerLogger — Type
MuTunerLogger{T<:AbstractFloat}

Type to assist with dynamically tuning the chemical potential $\mu$ to achieve a target particle density $\langle n \rangle$.

Fields

  • n₀::AbstractFloat

    Target filling as intensive value $\langle n \rangle$.

  • N₀::AbstractFloat

    Target filling as exensive quantity $\langle N \rangle = V \times \langle n \rangle$.

  • β::AbstractFloat

    Inverse temperature.

  • V::Int64

    System size.

  • U₀::AbstractFloat

    Intensive energy scale.

  • α::AbstractFloat

    Extensive energy scale $\alpha = N / U_0$.

  • c::AbstractFloat

    Fraction of history to discard when calculating averages.

  • μₜ₊₁::AbstractFloat

    The next chemical potential value for time t+1.

  • μ_bar::AbstractFloat

    The forgetful mean of the chemical potential $\bar{\mu}_t = \frac{1}{L_t} \sum_{t' = \lceil t/c \rceil}^t \mu_{t'}$ where $L_t = t - \lceil t/c \rceil + 1$.

  • μ_var::AbstractFloat

    The forgetful variance of the chemical potential.

  • N_bar::AbstractFloat

    The forgetful mean of the total particle number $\bar{N}_t = \frac{1}{L_t} \sum_{t' = \lceil t/c \rceil}^t N_{t'}$.

  • N_var::AbstractFloat

    The forgetful variance of the total particle number.

  • N²_bar::AbstractFloat

    The forgetful mean of the square of total particle number $\overline{N^2}_t = \frac{1}{L_t} \sum_{t' = \lceil t/c \rceil}^t N^2_{t'}$.

  • κ_bar::AbstractFloat

    The forgetful mean of the compressibility $\bar{\kappa}_t = \frac{1}{L_t} \sum_{t' = \lceil t/c \rceil}^t \kappa_{t'}$.

  • μ_traj::Vector{T} where T<:AbstractFloat

    Sequence $\mu_t$ of chemical potential values.

  • N_traj::Vector{T} where T<:AbstractFloat

    Sequence $N_t$ of total particle number values.

  • N²_traj::Vector{T} where T<:AbstractFloat

    Sequence $N^2_t$ of total particle number squared values.

MuTunerLogger(n₀::T, β::T, V::Int, U₀::T=1.0, c::T=0.5, μ₀::T=0.0, record::Bool=false)
    where {T<:AbstractFloat}

Constructs an instance of MuTunerLogger.

Arguments

  • n₀::T: Target particle density $\langle n \rangle$.
  • β::T: Inverse temperature.
  • V::Int: System size.
  • U₀::T=1.0: Characteristic intensive energy scale.
  • c::T=0.5: Fraction of history to discard when calculating forgetful averages and variances.
  • μ₀::T=0.0: Initial guess for chemical potential.
